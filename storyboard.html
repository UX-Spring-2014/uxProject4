Storyboard

Overview of entire storyboard
  • Idea
  • Notes
  • Collaborate
  • Write
  • Review
  • Editor Review
  • Distribute
  • Fan Feedback / Follow-up
Idea [clockwise]
  • Svetlana reads a scholarly journal and a scholarly book
  • Some unknown amount of time passes (a few weeks or something)
  • As she is walking along a path, she has a new (light bulb) idea
  • Svetlana starts the InkHorn app.
  • It's the first launch, so it knows its a new project. Starts project on InkHorn with an audio note
  • The project is created and stored on the InkHorn server, accessible everywhere
  • Time goes by again (unknown duration)
Collect Notes [counter-clockwise]
  • Svetlana is walking by an object, and notices and likes it
  • She launches the native mobile picture app
  • She aims the phone, and takes the picture
  • She presses the 'InkHorn' button, and it syncs with InkHorn (via cloud)
  • Some more time goes by.
  • In a cab ride on the way home, She is talking about a subtopic of the idea to a cab driver who mentioned a related idea.
  • She opens InkHorn
  • She selects 'Transcription' to auto scribe the speech to text
Collect Notes (continued) [clockwise]
  • It begins to auto transcribe, leaving space for annotations, and marking unknown speech in a lighter color.
  • She adds a note that he was smiling when he said 'I loved it'
  • She stops the recording, and it auto saves to the cloud storage, with auto generated meta data
  • At home, she researches some scholarly articles and 'stores/saves' them in 'Notes' section of InkHorn
Collaboration
  • She realizes that she will need some help to write the scholarly article, and needs some collaboration
  • She sends an email to request a collaborator from a friend, Mergatroyd
Organize Notes
  • Some time goes by, but she has so much work to get done, so she continues to organize all of the notes she has accumulated
  • She opens the 'Notes' sections and see all the notes she has
Organize Notes (continued) [down]
  • She gets a notification that she has a new collaborating author, Mergatroyd
  • She opens the project in the writing view
  • She drags in the notes to organize them on the canvas around the document section
  • She writes an outline, and links the notes to the specific section(s) in the document
  • She moves notes around on the canvas
  • And adds written annotations
Writing [down]
  • Svetlana starts writing on one section
  • She skips to another section and adds some writing there
  • Mergatroyd (in another location) works by writing in a different section
Review [clockwise]
  • Svetlana starts reviewing part of the paper, and makes a few edits and notations
  • Reviewing the same portion, Mergatroyd makes a few edits, and Svetlana sees it in real time
  • Svetlana begins working on another section, and wants Mergatroyd to look at a specific section while they are both 'online'
  • Svetlana sends a 'Summon' request to Mergatroyd (can also summon all people online, and also can 'stalk' another user, or 'ping')
  • Mergatroyd gets a notification that Svetlana wants help in a section, and he agrees.
  • Mergatroyd's viewing area becomes the same area and they work on it for a few minutes and then separate
Review (continued) [counter-clockwise]
  • Svetlana decides its good enough to ask for a review from another collaborator, their advisor
  • Svetlana sends a message asking for review of a certain portion
  • Over time....
  • The Advisor gets a message to review the work document (similar to Mergatroyd)
  • The Advisor makes notes
  • The ADvisor sends the review back to Svetlana and Mergatroyd
  • Svetlana sees the notification, and clicks on it, taking her to the lists of things to be addressed, and deadlines beside ones that have them
Review (continued...) [up]
  • The list item is sortable, but because it is short, she goes directly to the writing view
  • Svetlana can see the edits as well in the document writing view
  • Svetlana makes edits and comments further on the review suggestions, and marks any tasks/list items as complete
  • Svetlana clicks 'Communications' to discuss edits with Mergatroyd
Review (continued...) [up]
  • Mergatroyd gets a message to follow up on a review
  • As times goes by, and likely several other communications, reviews, and edits occur, the document gets to a better state
  • Svetlana likes the state it is at, and 'Polls' for agreement
  • Mergatroyd gets a notification (through InkHorn Cloud) and agrees that it is at a decent stage
  • The Advisor gets a notification (through InkHorn Cloud) and agrees that it is at a decent stage
  • InkHorn automatically creates an appropriate format document for the editor to review
Editor Review [up]
  • Editor receives notification that they have a new document to review
Editor Review [up]
  • Editor reviews and makes edits
  • Editor sends feedback/comments/concerns
Editor Review [up]
  • InkHorn collects the information, and notifies Svetlana, Mergatroyd, and the Advisor
  • The notification is clicked, taking them to the 'TODO' list, with deadlines
Review [clockwise]
  • Each author makes their own edits, marking them off the TODO list once done
  • They can use all the features previously discussed
  • Mergatroyd believes it is good enough for a second submission back to the editor, and 'Polls' everyone
  • Upon confirmation, InkHorn bundles everything and sends it back to the Editor
Editor Review / Review [clockwise]
  • The Editor receives another notification about a second review
  • This 'Review' cycle may continue many times
      • If it does, the cycle will include
    • local edits between the authors and Advisor
    • revisions between the author and the Editor
    • Multiple versions are tracked in InkHorn
  • Once a 'final version' is thought to exist, Svetlana 'Polls' and upon agreement, InkHorn generates the final versions in the correct format(s) for the editor
Distribution [counter-clockwise]
  • Over time, the Editor handles the distribution of the different formats to the different 'Readers' or communities
  • Svetlana is very interested in seeing her document throughout its distribution, so she logs on to the 'Statistics' view and looks at the statistics surrounding the status of the distribution
  • The Article is distributed to a conference website (immediately), conference magazine (in the next few months), to a book (next season), and to industry experts (immediately to soon)
  • The article has public facing contact information that is linked through InkHorn
  • Readers read the article, with varying opinions
Fan feedback and Follow-up [clockwise]
  • A fan likes the article, and sends an email to the contact info in the article
  • InkHorn captures this information and notifies All, and Svetlana decides to respond
  • She adds a note to the project 'Notes' Section, and replies to the email
  • The note is stored for the project, and can be the start of another version or a sequel, starting the process all over again.
